How Much Does Lawn Care Cost in Milton, Oakville & Mississauga? (2026 Guide)

BlackHorse Property Care June 15, 2026 6 min read

"How much is this going to cost me?" is the first question every homeowner asks — and the one most lawn care companies dodge. So let's answer it honestly. Prices below reflect what professional lawn care typically runs in Milton, Oakville and Mississauga in 2026. Your exact quote depends on your property, but this will tell you what's reasonable and what's a red flag.

Typical lawn care prices in the GTA-West

Lawn mowing & edging for a standard suburban lot usually runs $40–$80 per visit, with most homes on a weekly or bi-weekly schedule through the growing season. Larger lots, corner lots and heavily landscaped yards sit at the higher end.

Spring or fall cleanups are bigger one-time jobs — clearing leaves, debris, dead growth and refreshing beds. Expect $150–$450 depending on yard size and how much the season left behind.

Core aeration typically costs $80–$150, and pairing it with overseeding usually adds $50–$100 for the seed and application. It's the single best value in lawn care — more on that below.

Mulch installation is usually priced per cubic yard installed, commonly $100–$150 per yard including material, delivery and spreading.

Driveway sealing for a standard two-car asphalt driveway generally runs $150–$300, varying with square footage and the condition of the surface.

Rule of thumb: if a quote is dramatically cheaper than these ranges, ask what's being skipped — insurance, proper equipment, edging, or cleanup of clippings. In lawn care, "cheap" usually shows up in your yard within a month.

The five things that actually move your quote

  1. Lot size and shape. The biggest factor. A pie-shaped corner lot with islands and beds takes far longer than a simple rectangle of the same area.
  2. Condition. An overgrown or neglected yard needs a restoration visit before regular pricing applies. Bringing a lawn back costs more than maintaining one.
  3. Access. Gated backyards, slopes, and yards where equipment can't easily reach add time, and time is what you're paying for.
  4. Frequency. Weekly service is cheaper per visit than on-demand calls, because a maintained lawn cuts faster and predictable routes cost less to run.
  5. What's included. Some companies quote a low mow price, then bill extra for edging, trimming and blowing off hard surfaces. Always ask what a "visit" includes.

Why upfront pricing matters more than the lowest price

The most common complaint we hear from new customers isn't about price — it's about surprises. A vague estimate that grows once the work is done, or an invoice with line items nobody mentioned. Whoever you hire, get the number in writing before the crew shows up. (It's why we quote every job upfront: no surprises on the invoice is a policy, not a slogan.)

Is professional lawn care worth it?

Do the math on your own time. A typical mow-edge-trim-cleanup cycle takes a homeowner 60–90 minutes weekly, plus mower fuel, maintenance and the trimmer that never starts. Over a 26-week season, that's 30–40 hours. For many families in Milton, Oakville and Mississauga, handing that to a crew that does it faster and sharper is one of the cheapest hours-back-in-your-week purchases available.
